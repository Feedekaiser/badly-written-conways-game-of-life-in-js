What is Conway's Game of Life?

Conway's Game of Life is "zero-player" game devised in 1970 by the british mathematician John Horton Conway that takes place on a grid of cells. Each cell is either "alive" or "dead". The game evolves over time or not based on a set of rules.

The rules are simple:

  • If a living cell has 1 or 0 living neighbour, it dies of loneliness. Guess it doesn't pay to be anti-social.
  • If a living cell has more than 3 living neighbour, it dies of overcrowding. I told you they needed some personal space!
  • If a dead cell has exactly 3 living neighbors, it comes to life. Looks like three's company, but four's a crowd - for dead cells, at least.
The game begins with an initial pattern of living and dead cells, and then the rules are applied over and over again to create new patterns. Sometimes these patterns die out quickly, while other times they grow and evolve into complex shapes and structures.
